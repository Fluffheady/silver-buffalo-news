Silver Buffalo News Editorial Team

We're Your Neighbors.
We've Lived This Too.

Three retired Buffalo professionals who got tired of seeing seniors in WNY miss out on money, health knowledge, and the stories that matter most. So we started writing them ourselves.

Editor-in-Chief

Jim "Sully" Sullivan

Finance · Consumer Rights · Local Scams · Veterans

Jim Sullivan spent 31 years as an investigative reporter in Buffalo before retiring to South Buffalo, where he still keeps a scanner on his kitchen counter. He co-founded Silver Buffalo News because someone had to — and because retirement was making him crazy.

Sully covers the money stuff: benefit programs the county doesn't advertise, Medicare scams targeting WNY seniors, and anything that smells like someone is taking advantage of the people he grew up with in this city. He's blunt. He's protective. And he does not like to waste your time.

Health & Lifestyle Editor

Barb Miller

Longevity · Sleep · Hearing · Wellness · Walking

Barb Miller is a retired RN who spent 28 years at Erie County Medical Center and still volunteers there on Tuesdays. She lives in Amherst with her husband and is grandmother to four kids who she claims eat too much processed food.

When Barb started reading what passed for "senior health advice" online, she just about threw her laptop. The articles were either written by people who'd never set foot in a hospital, or buried in jargon that assumed you had a medical degree. She writes the health section the way she talked to her patients: plain, practical, and honest.

❤️ Health & Longevity 😴 Sleep 👂 Hearing 🚶 Walking & Fitness 🥗 Nutrition
"I'm not going to tell you to 'consult your doctor' and leave it at that. You deserve to actually understand what's happening in your body."
Nostalgia & Home Editor

Vinnie DiPaolo

Collectibles · DIY · Buffalo History · Home Prep

Vinnie DiPaolo renovated over 400 homes across Erie County in a 35-year contracting career. Now retired, he spends his time at estate sales, writing about Buffalo history, and arguing with his sons about the Bills' offensive line.

Vinnie knows every Victorian on the West Side and can tell you which furnace brand is still running after 25 Buffalo winters. His nostalgia pieces started as long emails to his kids about "the way things were" — and when his daughter told him he should write for other people, he figured why not. He knows his stuff.

"I've been in basements and attics all over this city. Half of what gets thrown out at estate sales around here is worth real money. I'm just trying to tell people before it goes to the dumpster."

Why We Started This

We didn't start Silver Buffalo News because we wanted to be in media. We started it because we kept seeing the same thing: people who'd spent 35 years working in this city reaching their 60s and 70s without knowing what they were entitled to. Programs that existed, benefits that went unclaimed, scams that worked because nobody was warning people.

This isn't a corporate outlet. Nobody owns us. We cover what matters to people who live in WNY — and we say what we actually think.

— Sully, Barb & Vinnie · Buffalo, NY · 2026

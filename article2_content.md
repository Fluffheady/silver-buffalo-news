## Program #1: HEAP — The Heating Bill Help Most People Don't Claim

**What it is:** The Home Energy Assistance Program (HEAP) helps income-eligible households pay their heating bills. In Erie County, this is run through the Erie County Department of Social Services at 460 Main Street, Buffalo.

Buffalo winters are some of the most expensive in the nation for heating. The average Erie County homeowner pays significantly more to heat their home than residents in states just a few hundred miles south. HEAP was created specifically to ease that burden.

**Who qualifies:** Your household's gross monthly income must be at or below the Erie County HEAP income guidelines. For a single-person household, the current monthly income limit is **$3,473**. For a two-person household, it is **$4,542**. If you receive SNAP benefits, Temporary Assistance, or Supplemental Security Income (SSI), you automatically qualify. You can own your home, have savings, or even have stocks and bonds — and still be eligible.

**How much you can get:** Benefits vary based on your heating source. Households that heat with oil, kerosene, or propane and pay directly to the vendor can receive **$900 or more** per year. Households that pay for heat as part of their rent can still receive $45–$50. There is also an Emergency HEAP benefit if you face a heating crisis mid-winter.

**How to apply in Buffalo:** Apply online at [mybenefits.ny.gov](https://www.mybenefits.ny.gov), by mail to ECDSS at 95 Franklin Street, Buffalo, NY 14202, by fax to (716) 858-6765, or in person at 460 Main Street, Monday–Friday, 8am–4pm. You can also email heaphotline@erie.gov and they will mail you a paper application. For questions, call the HEAP hotline at **716-858-7644**.

**Deadline:** The regular HEAP benefit typically opens in November/December and runs through spring. Cooling assistance opens in spring. Always verify current open dates with Erie County, as windows shift slightly each year.

## Program #2: EPIC — The Prescription Drug Savings New Yorkers Keep Missing

**What it is:** EPIC stands for Elderly Pharmaceutical Insurance Coverage. It is a New York State program that wraps around your Medicare Part D plan to reduce what you pay for prescription drugs.

Here is what makes EPIC unusual: the income eligibility ceiling is much higher than most people expect. **You can earn up to $75,000 per year as a single person, or $100,000 per year as a married couple**, and still qualify. Assets — savings, investments, property — are not counted at all.

**Who qualifies:** New York State residents age 65 or older who are enrolled in a Medicare Part D drug plan. Medicaid recipients are not eligible (they already have full coverage).

**What it pays for:** EPIC reduces your Medicare Part D co-payments to as little as $3–$20 per prescription, depending on the drug cost. For members with income below $23,000 (single) or $29,000 (married), EPIC also pays your Medicare Part D plan premium, up to the cost of a basic plan. EPIC also covers many drugs that Medicare Part D excludes entirely.

**How to apply:** You can apply at any time of the year — there is no enrollment window. Download the application from the New York State Department of Health website or call the EPIC toll-free helpline at **1-800-332-3742**, available Monday–Friday, 8:30am–5pm. No documentation is required with the application — EPIC verifies information directly with Social Security and the NYS Tax Department.

**Why people miss it:** Many seniors assume the income limit is low, hear the word "elderly" in the name, and assume it is for very low-income people only. It is not. A retired couple with a combined income of $95,000 can still qualify for meaningful prescription savings.

## Program #3: Enhanced STAR — The Property Tax Break Hiding on Your Tax Bill

**What it is:** The Enhanced STAR (School Tax Relief) exemption is a New York State property tax program that reduces the school tax portion of your property tax bill. For eligible seniors in Erie County, this can mean hundreds of dollars in annual savings on your property taxes.

**Who qualifies:** You must be 65 or older, own your home as your primary residence, and have combined household income at or below **$107,300** (based on 2023 income for the 2025 benefit year). This income limit increases slightly each year.

**How much it saves:** The Enhanced STAR exemption provides at least a $50,000 reduction in your property's assessed value for school tax purposes. For a median-priced Buffalo-area home, this translates to a school tax reduction of several hundred dollars per year. Many seniors also qualify for additional local senior exemptions on top of STAR — ask your local assessor.

**How to apply in Erie County:** Apply at your local assessor's office. In the City of Buffalo, contact the Buffalo Assessment Office. In most Erie County towns, the deadline is **March 1** each year. If you already receive the Basic STAR exemption and are turning 65 this year, apply to upgrade before that deadline. If you receive a STAR credit check instead of an exemption, New York State will upgrade you automatically if you qualify — no action needed.

**Important:** If you have never applied for any form of STAR and you own your home, stop reading right now and call your local assessor's office. You may have been leaving money on the table for years.

## Program #4: FeedMore WNY SNAP Enrollment Help — Free Groceries You May Already Qualify For

**What it is:** SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program, formerly food stamps) is a federal program that provides monthly grocery benefits loaded onto an EBT card. FeedMore WNY, headquartered at 100 James E. Casey Drive in Buffalo, offers **free SNAP outreach and enrollment assistance** specifically for Erie County adults who may qualify but have not yet applied.

**Who qualifies:** SNAP eligibility is based on income and household size. Many seniors who live alone on Social Security are surprised to find they qualify. Seniors 60 and older are also eligible for higher resource limits than younger applicants.

**What you get:** A single-person household can receive up to **$298 per month** in SNAP benefits. Benefits are loaded monthly onto an EBT card that works like a debit card at Tops, Wegmans, Aldi, and most other grocery stores.

**How to get help in Buffalo:** Call FeedMore WNY at **716-822-2005, extension 3052** to schedule a free appointment. Their SNAP outreach team will help you complete and submit your application at no cost to you. You can also apply directly through [mybenefits.ny.gov](https://www.mybenefits.ny.gov) or call Erie County at **716-858-7239**.

**Why people miss it:** SNAP still carries a stigma for the generation that grew up calling them food stamps. But SNAP today is used by millions of working and retired Americans, including many middle-class seniors whose fixed incomes do not stretch as far as they once did. There is no shame in claiming a benefit you are legally entitled to and have paid into through decades of taxes.

## Program #5: NY Connects — The Caregiver Lifeline in Erie County

**What it is:** NY Connects is a free information and referral service run by the Erie County Department for the Aging. It connects seniors and their family caregivers with long-term services and support — everything from home health aides to transportation, meal delivery, respite care, and caregiver counseling.

If you are caring for an aging spouse or parent, or if you are a senior trying to stay independent in your own home, NY Connects is the single most useful phone number in Erie County. Their staff navigates the complicated web of local, state, and federal programs so you do not have to.

**Who it helps:** Any Erie County resident who is older, has a disability, or is caring for someone who does. There is no income requirement and no application process. You simply call.

**What they can connect you to:** Home-delivered meals (Meals on Wheels), in-home personal care, transportation assistance, adult day programs, caregiver support groups, legal aid referrals, benefits counseling, and much more. They can also help you apply for HEAP, EPIC, and SNAP if you have not done so.

**How to reach NY Connects in Erie County:** Call **716-858-8526** or toll-free at **800-396-2705**, Monday–Friday, 8:30am–4:30pm. You can also email nyconnects@erie.gov. The office is located at 95 Franklin Street, 13th floor, Buffalo, NY 14202.

**The hidden value:** Many families wait until a crisis — a fall, a hospitalization, a caregiver burning out — to call NY Connects. Do not wait. Call now to understand what is available, so you have a plan before you need one.

## Program #6: AARP Smart Driver Course — Up to 10% Off Your Car Insurance for 3 Years

**What it is:** The AARP Smart Driver course is a driver safety refresher program, available online or in a classroom, that qualifies you for a mandatory auto insurance discount under New York State law.

New York is one of 33 states where auto insurers are **legally required** to offer premium discounts to drivers who complete a state-approved driver improvement course. AARP's course qualifies. AARP members get an additional 10% discount on the course itself.

**Who qualifies:** Any licensed driver in New York State. For seniors, the insurance discount typically applies for **three years** after course completion, after which you can retake the course and renew the discount. Drivers age 55 and older are specifically targeted by the program.

**How much you can save:** Discounts vary by insurer, but the standard discount is up to **10% on your auto insurance premium** for three years. On a $1,200/year policy, that is $360 in savings over the three-year period — for a course that takes a few hours and costs around $20–$25.

**How to enroll:** Visit [aarpdriversafety.org](https://www.aarpdriversafety.org) to take the course online, or find a local classroom session near Buffalo through the AARP website. You do not need to be an AARP member to take the course, though members receive a discount on the fee. After completing the course, present your completion certificate to your insurance company and request the discount.

**Check before June 30:** Many insurers apply discounts at your next renewal. If your policy renews before summer, completing the course now ensures you capture the discount on your next renewal. Verify the exact timing with your insurance agent.

## Don't Leave This Money on the Table

These six programs are not charity. They are funded by tax dollars — your tax dollars, paid in over decades of working in Western New York. Claiming them is exactly what they were designed for.

A single Erie County senior who qualifies for all six of these programs could realistically save **$3,000–$5,000 or more per year** — money that stays in your pocket instead of going toward utility bills, prescription co-pays, grocery costs, and property taxes.

The easiest way to start: call **NY Connects at 716-858-8526**. Tell them you want to know what programs you qualify for. They will walk you through the rest.

Always verify current eligibility rules and deadlines directly with the administering agency, as income limits and program windows change annually.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I have to be very low income to qualify for these programs?

No. Several of these programs have surprisingly high income ceilings. EPIC, for example, covers single seniors earning up to $75,000 per year and married couples up to $100,000. Enhanced STAR covers households with income up to $107,300. Even HEAP, which is more income-limited, covers single-person households earning up to $3,473 per month in gross income. Check each program individually rather than assuming you do not qualify.

### Can I apply for multiple programs at the same time?

Yes, and you should. These programs are designed to stack — you can receive HEAP, EPIC, STAR, and SNAP simultaneously. NY Connects can help you navigate enrollment in multiple programs through a single point of contact. FeedMore WNY's SNAP outreach team can also refer you to other programs while helping you with your SNAP application.

### I already get Medicare. Does that affect my eligibility for EPIC?

EPIC is designed to work *with* Medicare Part D, not replace it. You must be enrolled in a Medicare Part D drug plan to receive EPIC benefits. EPIC then reduces the out-of-pocket costs you pay under that plan. Having Medicare actually helps with EPIC eligibility — it does not exclude you.

### What if I missed the HEAP deadline this year?

If the regular HEAP benefit window has closed, you may still qualify for Emergency HEAP if you face a heating crisis. Cooling assistance benefits also open in spring. Check the current status at [www3.erie.gov/heap](https://www3.erie.gov/heap) or call 716-858-7644. Mark your calendar for November — that is when the regular benefit window typically reopens.

### I am caring for a parent, not a senior myself. Does NY Connects help caregivers?

Yes. NY Connects specifically serves family caregivers and is a key part of the National Family Caregiver Support Program in Erie County. They can connect you to respite care (temporary relief from caregiving duties), support groups, counseling, and financial assistance programs for caregivers. Call 716-858-8526 and identify yourself as a caregiver.
